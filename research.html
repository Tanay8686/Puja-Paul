Molecular Dynamics Simulation of Alloying Characteristics of Al-Mg Nanoparticles under Different Process Heating Condition (Journal)

DOI: https://doi.org/10.1080/08927022.2024.2381583

Keywords: Al-Mg Alloy, Heating Rate, Molecular Dynamics, Coalescence Kinetics, Dislocation analysis, Stacking Fault

Under the supervision of Dr. Mohammad Nasim Hasan, this study investigates the effect of thermal process parameters on the alloying of the Al-10Mg (wt%) system using molecular dynamics. Five different heating rates were applied to Al and Mg nanoparticles (NPs) to observe melting, coalescence, and subsequent cooling. The research focused on alloying temperature, atomic migration, coalescence kinetics, and mechanical properties at various heating rates. Initial simulations determined the melting temperatures and thermo-stability of single particles (SPs) of Al and Mg. Results indicated that heating rates significantly affect the melting temperature of NPs. The alloying process included heating, relaxation, and cooling/solidification phases, followed by uniaxial tensile tests to assess mechanical behavior in context to dislocation analysis, HCP evolution, stacking fault analysis and corresponding stress-strain relationship.. While heating rates and coalescence kinetics showed a substantial relationship, they had minimal impact on mechanical properties.

Analysis of Effective Thermal Insulation Properties of Locally Available Jute Stick Boards (Undergraduate Thesis)

Thesis Supervisor: Dr. Anup Saha, Assistant Professor, Department of Mechanical Engineering, BUET

Keywords: Jute, Thermal Insulation, Lee’s Disc Method, Ansys Steady State Thermal Simulation

Jute, renowned for its affordability and versatile properties, presents an innovative alternative to foam-based thermal insulators. This thesis explores the potential of Jute Particle Board (JPB), a locally available and sustainable material, as a building insulation material. The research investigates JPB's thermal conductivity and its impact on indoor temperature through a preliminary numerical simulation. The Lee’s Disc Method is used for measuring the thermal conductivity of poorly conducting materials like jute composites and particle boards. The calculated thermal conductivity of JPB was 0.175559 W/m K which falls within the range of conventional particleboards, offering moderate insulation but not surpassing dedicated insulation materials. However, JPB demonstrates significantly higher thermal resistance compared to commonly used building materials like bricks and concrete blocks. This suggests JPB's potential as an additional insulating layer to improve building envelope performance. By demonstrating the feasibility and effectiveness of jute stick-based systems, this research paves the way for sustainable and cost-efficient solutions in diverse industries, promoting the use of environmentally friendly materials in construction and design. Additionally, numerical modeling was employed to assess the performance of JPB in a typical Bangladeshi household setting utilizing Ansys steady state thermal analysis. The successful implementation of JPB insulation could lead to reduced reliance on imported materials, promotion of local resources and improved energy efficiency in Bangladeshi homes.

Thermal Management of Outer Shell of a Coaxial Cylindrical Shell Contaiing a Constant Flux Heat Source Using P, PI & PID Controller

Keywords: Galerkin finite element method, Flow control, PID controller, Transient response, Coaxial cylinders

Under the supervision of Dr. Sumon Saha, this research delves into the utilization of Proportional (P), Proportional-Integral (PI), and Proportional Integral-Derivative (PID) controllers in the context of coaxial cylinders to regulate the flow of cooling water within the annular cavity, aiming to efficiently cool the outer shell of the cylinder. The study systematically examines the impact of these control methodologies on critical performance metrics including steady-state error, overshoot, and transient response. Employing a combination of simulation techniques and practical experimentation, we meticulously assess the efficacy of each controller type in maintaining desired cooling conditions while mitigating deviations from target setpoints. Our comprehensive analysis reveals distinct operational characteristics and trade-offs associated with P, PI, and PID controllers, shedding light on their suitability for diverse industrial cooling applications. These findings contribute valuable insights for optimizing the design and implementation of cooling systems, fostering enhanced performance and energy efficiency in industrial processes

Effect Of Powder Bed Fusion Process Parameters On Microstructural And Mechanical Properties Of Random Sized High/Medium Entropy Alloy

Keywords: Additive Manufacturing, Powder bed Fusion, Random Sized Particle

In this project with Dr. Mohammad Nasim Hasan in the field of Additive Manufacturing, we propose a molecular dynamics simulation of the powder bed fusion process, involving multiple randomly-sized particles distributed on a solid copper bed following a Weibull distribution. Each particle is modeled with a core-shell structure, coated with another material. The system is subjected to heating via a LASER beam to simulate the additive manufacturing process, with a focus on varying LASER parameters such as speed, direction, and power. After heating, the system will be cooled to room temperature, and mechanical tests will be conducted on the resolidified alloy. This study aims to investigate the formation of voids within the resolidified alloy and assess their impact on the alloy's mechanical properties.
